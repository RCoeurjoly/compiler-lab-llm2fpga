## Interpretation for the canonical RC

The canonical route remains the frozen PT2E W8A8 RC through Torch-MLIR/MLIR/CIRCT without a semantic replacement. This screen found no upstream compiler pass, Calyx/HardFloat library primitive, or paper artifact that constitutes a direct, canonical `math.exp` lowerer for that route.

The five rows labelled `direct implementation evidence` establish that real FPGA projects compose exponentiation, reduction, normalization, and scheduling in working accelerator designs. They do **not** establish a drop-in MLIR/Calyx primitive, a numerical contract matching the frozen RC, or a proof about PT2E W8A8 observables. The `approximation or co-design evidence` rows instead make the semantic change explicit: LUTs, piecewise functions, low-precision formats, and polynomial/Taylor methods require their own chosen contract and later oracle evidence. Literature evidence does not prove PT2E equivalence.

Therefore this corpus is evidence for the next research choice, not approval to transform the RC. Any noncanonical composition remains a separately approved candidate and must pass the future RC hardware-oracle gate before it can claim equivalence.
